## Before the Story Begins — Background and Prehistory

These scenes establish the world before Ned arrives at Venn Court.

### 1. Edmund at the Stone (Before Ned)
**File:** `prose_edmund_at_the_stone.md`
**When:** Years before Ned arrives. A typical early morning visit to the stone.
**Viewpoint:** Edmund
**Notes:** Establishes Edmund's private relationship with the stone — the warmth, the birds, the quality of time. His awareness that he has been writing around this his entire career without writing about it directly. *The most extraordinary thing he knows.*

## Movement One — Ned's Journey and Arrival (Mid-July 2026)

### 2. Ned's Last Night Before Venn Court
**File:** `prose_last_night_before_venn_court.md`
**When:** The night before Ned leaves for Somerset. Great-Aunt Marian's house.
**Viewpoint:** Ned (with Marian's interior at the close)
**Notes:** The china dish moving six inches to the left. Ned moving it back with both hands — practiced, automatic. The photograph of the husband turning. The compass pointing north, as it always does. Marian's final thought: *she hoped he would find somewhere it was all right to carry it.*

### 3. Ned's First Impression of Somerset
**File:** `prose_ned_first_impression_of_somerset.md`
**When:** The train journey. London Paddington to Castle Cary.
**Viewpoint:** Ned
**Notes:** The Somerset Levels seen from the train window. *Old. It's just very, very old.* The feeling of being noticed — by the land itself, perhaps. The notebooks almost opened but not. *Somerset watched him back.*

### 4. Chips at Castle Cary — The First Meeting
**File:** `prose_chips_at_castle_cary.md`
**When:** Castle Cary station. Mid-July.
**Viewpoint:** Edmund (primarily)
**Notes:** The piece of paper that says NED — unnecessary, endearing. Edmund noticing Ned's slight undernourishment and suggesting the chip shop without making anything of it. Ned's *all right* rather than *yes please*. The Somerset Levels, *it feels older*, *no one does, entirely.* The silence that establishes itself between them like a small but useful thing.

### 5. Ned Arrives at Venn Court
**File:** `prose_ned_arrives_at_venn_court.md`
**When:** The afternoon of arrival. Mid-July.
**Viewpoint:** Ned
**Notes:** The lane narrowing. The house — layered old. The kitchen. Ned's system for new places beginning to fail him immediately. Edmund's *you may read anything you find here.* The three books on the shelf — Edmund's childhood books, not bought for the occasion. Ned going to the books before the window. The backpack set down on the bed.

## The First Weeks (Late July 2026)

### 6. The First Night — The Bedtime Reading (Revised)
**File:** `prose_first_bedtime_reading.md`
**When:** Ned's first night at Venn Court.
**Viewpoint:** Both — dual viewpoint
**Notes:** Ned's practiced nighttime vigilance — not childish fear but something specific. Edmund's ease in his own house at night, which Ned has never encountered before. *The Little Prince* passed back and forth. Ned's fractional pause on *this hasn't much improved my opinion of them.* The tawny owl. Ned falling asleep mid-passage. *The sleep of a boy who had, for one night at least, put his watchfulness down.*

### 7. The Walk to Somerton and Finding the Bicycle
**File:** `prose_walk_to_somerton.md`
**When:** First or second week. Mid-July.
**Viewpoint:** Edmund (primarily)
**Notes:** Mrs. Perrett and the pastry without being asked. Derek in the hardware shop. Ned watching local children on bicycles. The green bicycle found, the helmet from Derek's shop. The ride home — Ned circling back, unable to leave Edmund behind. *Thinking about nothing in particular, which was becoming somewhat easier since Ned had arrived.*

### 8. Ned and the Kitchen Garden
**File:** `prose_ned_kitchen_garden.md`
**When:** Second week onward. Establishing across several weeks.
**Viewpoint:** Ned and Edmund
**Notes:** Vilmorin-Andrieux produced and handed over with complete seriousness. Ned taking it under his arm to the garden. The carrots, the fork with the short handle. Edmund watching from the library window. The pear scene — engravings becoming recognition, knowledge becoming belonging. Ned: *I'll be here in September.* Edmund: *I know.*

## The Central Summer Sequence (Late July — August 2026)

### 9. The First Visit to the Stone (Revised)
**File:** `prose_first_visit_to_the_stone.md`
**When:** Approximately day 14. Late July.
**Viewpoint:** Ned (primarily)
**Notes:** Ned asks *where do you go?* The orchard, the meadow, the stone appearing above the grass. The warmth — unexpected, dry. Ned noticing that *nothing is wrong* — the strangest place he has ever been and no familiar dread. The compass not pointing north — filed away, not yet shown. Edmund's monologue — the first time he has said any of this aloud. Ned: *I think you're right.* Edmund hearing the specific quality of that certainty. *I don't know yet* — and Edmund hearing the difference. The touch on the shoulder. Later: *Venn Court* written at the top of a fresh page.

### 10. The Ceremony — A Day's Vignette
**File:** `prose_vignette_the_ceremony.md`
**When:** Approximately days 17-18. Late July.
**Viewpoint:** Both
**Notes:** Aunt Marian's letter at breakfast — Ned volunteering something personal, a small new thing. Edmund taking down Machen in the morning. The beans, the carrots, Vilmorin on storage. *This is a Machen story about a standing stone. Short.* Ned reading it that afternoon. *She was afraid of it.* / *I wasn't.* / *It felt old. Patient.* Edmund: *closer to what I think than anything I've read, including things I've written myself.* Blackwood's *Tarn of Sacrifice* introduced. Ned smelling the lamb from the garden at four o'clock — spontaneous, offered without being asked.

### 11. Edmund Asks Ned About School
**File:** `prose_edmund_asks_ned.md`
**When:** Approximately day 28. Early August.
**Viewpoint:** Edmund (primarily)
**Notes:** Over tea, Thursday evening. Edmund's careful framing. Ned's question that is not quite a question: *What happens at the end of summer?* Edmund: *I am not registering you at a school in order to remove you from it in July.* Ned's *all right* — quiet and considered. His *thank you* said to his tea, the only way he can say it yet. The wood pigeon outside, still enquiring.

### 12. The Second Visit to the Stone
**File:** `prose_second_visit_to_the_stone.md`
**When:** Approximately days 21-25. Late July to early August.
**Viewpoint:** Ned (primarily) with Edmund's interior at the close
**Notes:** Ned initiates — the first time. *You've been looking at the same page since I came in.* At the stone: *more awake* / *that's not the right word.* Ned's observation about the difference between being ignored and being asleep. The compass shown without words. *It thinks the stone is where north should be.* The technical papers requested. Ned's dryness about Will's internet research — first time he has been wry with Edmund. Edmund alone at the stone's boulders for a moment after: *The boy is ahead of him in some ways.*

### 13. Three Scenes: The Papers, The Stone, The Door
**File:** `prose_three_scenes_papers_stone_door.md`
**When:** Approximately days 22-35. Early to mid August.
**Viewpoint:** Scene 1 — both. Scene 2 — Edmund. Scene 3 — both.
**Notes:** Three scenes forming one movement.

**Scene 1 — The Technical Papers (Kitchen table)**
Ned with Lethbridge and the magnetometer studies. *That's basically everything about standing stones.* Ned's targeted question about Edmund's watch. Edmund confirming. The kitchen lamp mentioned. Ned's *sorry* said to the paper — automatic, reflex. Edmund: *don't be.* Quiet and complete.

**Scene 2 — Edmund at the Stone (Early morning)**
Edmund alone, processing. His private theory solidifying — the boy carries something that has had nowhere to go, and at Venn Court the stone receives it. The smell of toast as he walks back. Ned is up.

**Scene 3 — Conversation Five (Kitchen, evening)**
*Doesn't it bother you? The compass.* Edmund's equanimity — thirty years. *What else have you observed?* Ned's first disclosure: *sometimes things move. In places that don't feel right.* / *This place feels right.* Edmund mentions the lamp and his watch. Ned: *You noticed.* Ned: *It happened in every house.* Edmund: *not tonight. Tonight you've said enough.* Ned's second *thank you*, said to his book. Both reading until the kitchen is dark.

**Note:** This sequence reaches the threshold of Conversation Six (Ned's fuller disclosure) but deliberately stops before it. Conversation Six belongs in the school term.

## The Transition and Term Time (Late August — Autumn 2026)

### 14. The Last Night of The Little Prince, and What Came After
**File:** `prose_little_prince_end_lotr_begin.md`
**When:** Late July through into August (the reading); early August (the transition).
**Viewpoint:** Both
**Notes:** *He goes home. Whether that's the same thing is left open.* Ned: *he misses his rose. Even though she's vain and difficult.* Edmund not showing the new book that night — *let this one settle.* Two nights later: *The Fellowship of the Ring* produced from behind his back. Ned finding the map immediately. Edmund: *we'll be at it well into the school term.* Something passing briefly across Ned's face — quieter than relief. *You start.* Three weeks in: Ned asking whether Tolkien actually knew, about old things in the landscape. Edmund's answer — the shape of it, yes. Ned: *That's what you were doing. At the stone.*

### 16. The School Project — Somerset Prehistoric Monuments
**File:** `prose_school_project.md`
**When:** October 2026. A few weeks into the school term.
**Viewpoint:** Ned (primarily), Edmund at the close of Day Three
**Notes:** Three days, three movements. Mr Ashby's project assignment: *choose something from the history of your local area.* Ned chooses prehistoric monuments of Somerset before he has left the building.

**Day One — Stanton Drew:** Ned at the kitchen table with Miss Pardew's books, discovering Stanton Drew — second largest stone circle in Britain, older than anything he has a proper frame for, stones in a pub garden. Edmund sitting down to answer properly. The idea that the stone at Venn Court was part of a conversation across the landscape — *what were they saying? / We don't know. But they were saying something.* Ned beginning the Somerset map. Edmund: *we can go on Saturday.*

**Day Two — The Sweet Track:** The arithmetic of five thousand years — the Sweet Track older than the pyramids by thirteen hundred years. Ned working it out and needing a moment with the ceiling. The OS map produced from Edmund's library — Edmund has three copies of the same sheet, each for different purposes. Finding the monument symbol on the map — the stone officially recorded, marked in Gothic script. The grid reference copied into Ned's notebook. *The real and the private, the official and the felt, on the same page.*

**Day Three — Saturday at Stanton Drew:** Edmund drives. The limestone villages, the same stone as Venn Court. At the Great Circle: the scale of it, even fallen. The compass behaving differently — hunting, uncertain, distributed rather than concentrated. Edmund noting the distinction between distributed and concentrated anomaly. The Cove in the pub garden — Ned's sketch of the three stones. Ned asking whether the people who built the pub knew what the stones were. Edmund: *people have always known, on some level, that these things are not to be disturbed.* On the drive home: Ned's project question — *why do people keep coming back, even when they've forgotten what the sites were for.* Edmund alone in the car: *a question that had kept him interested for thirty years being posed as a school project by a nine year old boy.*

**The buried detail:** Ned says *the stone at home* in passing, meaning the stone in the meadow. Edmund hears it. Neither of them says anything about it.
