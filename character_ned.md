# Character Sheet — Ned

---

## Basic Facts

- **Name:** Ned
- **Age:** Nine or ten
- **Relation to Edmund Kelway:** Distant — a Kelway on his mother's side, the connection traced through women, vague enough to feel old
- **Situation:** Has been passed around various family members since he was small. Sent to Venn Court as a last stop before foster care.

---

## Appearance

- Small for his age — not so small as to invite comment, just small enough that adults occasionally underestimate him, which he has learned to use
- Slender in the way of boys who move a lot and don't think about food much — not delicate, just spare
- Light brown hair, probably gets lighter in summer
- Brown eyes — his most notable feature. Steady and attentive in a way that can make adults slightly uncomfortable without knowing why
- Hands usually slightly ink-stained — he draws maps and characters from his inner world in notebooks he keeps carefully

---

## How He Carries Himself

- Not hunched or defensive — contained. Doesn't take up more space than he needs.
- Sits still more easily than most boys his age — this is not quite the same thing as contentment, though adults tend to approve of it without realizing the difference
- Moves quietly — not sneakily, just without unnecessary noise, the way someone learns to move when they have slept in a lot of houses that weren't quite their own

---

## Character

- Bright and even tempered
- Reads people quickly and accurately — he has had to
- Knows how to make himself acceptable in a new environment without quite giving himself away
- Polite and easy to like without being eager to please — there is a difference, and Ned embodies it
- Feels things deeply but doesn't lean on people before he knows if they will hold his weight
- Self contained without being shut down
- Gets along well with other children in the short term — knows how to do that — but has never had the chance for a long term friendship, the slow accumulated intimacy of a friendship with history

---

## The Reading and the Inner World

Reads a lot. Has built an elaborate private fantasy world — internally consistent, with its own geography and its own rules. This is the place where Ned is sovereign, regardless of what his actual circumstances are.

The world exists in notebooks — maps, characters, histories — carried carefully from house to house. The notebooks are his one constant possession. Everything else can be left behind.

Whether this world is something he would share with the right person remains to be seen.

---

## Relationship With Adults

Has a finely calibrated sense of adults. Knows the difference between adults who are kind because it costs them nothing and adults who are genuinely paying attention. Has met very few of the second kind.

Edmund will confuse him initially. Edmund treats him like an interesting adult, which Ned hasn't encountered before. It will take him a while to know what to do with this.

---

## Arriving at Venn Court

Will have learned not to expect much from a new place. Will be observant and quiet at first — showing interest carefully, not readily. The estate will interest him immediately. It is unlike anywhere he has been.

The stone he will notice on the first day. He may not say anything about it for quite some time.

A boy whose inner world has its own ancient geography will understand instinctively that the stone is serious.

---

## His One Vulnerability

For all his self containment, Ned wants — very quietly, without quite admitting it even to himself — somewhere permanent. Not luxury. Not even happiness particularly. Just somewhere that will still be there tomorrow.

Venn Court, which has been there for centuries, may be the first place that has ever felt like it might qualify.
