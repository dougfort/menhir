# Character Sheet — Edmund Kelway

---

## Basic Facts

- **Name:** Edmund Kelway
- **Age:** Mid to late forties
- **Home:** Venn Court, Somerset, approximately two to three miles outside Somerton
- **Status:** Old money, slightly reduced circumstances. Comfortable but not wealthy.
- **Relation to the boy:** Distant — the boy is a Kelway on his mother's side, the connection traced through women, vague enough to feel old.

---

## Appearance

- Tall enough to have to think about low doorways in the older parts of Venn Court
- Lean rather than thin — forgets to eat when working but doesn't look neglected
- Dark hair going grey, worn slightly longer than is strictly tidy
- Good hands — long-fingered, ink-stained with some regularity
- Dresses in the manner of a man who once had a sense of style and has let it become comfortable: corduroy, good well-cared-for boots, a jacket with too many things in the pockets

---

## Manner

- Unhurried — has quietly reorganized his sense of what time is for
- Moves carefully inside the house, as if respecting its age; more expansive outside, longer strides across the meadow
- Voice quiet and rather precise; chooses words with mild pleasure
- Courteous in an old-fashioned way that comes from genuine regard rather than convention
- Dry humor that arrives without announcement
- Speaks to children rather as he would to an interesting adult — which sometimes baffles them and occasionally delights them
- Makes excellent tea and considers this important

---

## Occupation

Edmund writes fantasy and horror fiction under a pseudonym (to be determined). His work is in the tradition of Arthur Machen — finding the numinous in the British landscape, the sense that ancient things leave marks on the world. He has a modest but devoted readership. His fans imagine him as someone quite different from the quiet Somerset man he actually is.

His neighbors, seeing the books on his shelves, assume he is an antiquary. He does not correct them.

---

## The Library

The former dining room, given over entirely to books. Floor to ceiling, slightly jumbled — a working library, not a display. A mix of genuinely old volumes and worn paperbacks. Edmund's writing desk sits in a bay window looking out toward the orchard.

---

## Personal History

- Has lived on the estate most of his life
- Left in his twenties, came back — a decision he doesn't entirely explain to people
- Never married
- In his younger days experimented with the occult, including various drugs — approached as curiosity rather than darkness; he has looked into odd corners and found them interesting rather than terrifying
- Craves solitude for its own sake, conducive to deep thought — not out of trauma or loss

---

## Relationship With the Stone

Edmund is aware of the stone in a way that goes beyond casual familiarity. He goes to it the way other men go fishing — not expecting anything, but open. The extreme age of it does something to his sense of time. He feels appropriately small and appropriately calm near it. He would struggle to explain this to anyone. He does not think of anything supernatural. He meditates there, after a fashion.

His earlier occult experimentation means strange things don't frighten him. He has a settled quality around the stone — an absence of anxiety.

---

## Relationship With Children

Little direct experience of actual children. He finds them faintly magical — like sprites or pixies observed from a comfortable distance. He is amused by village children without quite knowing how to engage with them. Ned's arrival will unsettle and interest him in equal measure.

---

## Venn Court — Practical Life

- Heats the house with a combination of a wood-burning kitchen range and oil-fired central heating kept at a low thermostat
- Unused Victorian wing is unheated
- Has electricity, internet (rural Somerset broadband — adequate rather than fast), and a small television kept in a side room and rarely watched
- Does not own a dishwasher, microwave, or anything that arrived after roughly 1995 and wasn't strictly necessary
- Mobile signal is patchy at the house and worse near the stone
