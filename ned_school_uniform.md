# Ned's School Uniform — A Supporting Note

---

## The School

The Somerton primary school is a small state school — the kind of Somerset institution that has been in the same building for generations, with a modest logo and colours chosen decades ago by someone who liked navy blue. The uniform is simple and inexpensive, available partly from the school office and partly from the supermarket in Taunton or online.

Edmund handles the purchasing without fuss in late August, shortly after registering Ned. He buys two of most things, on the grounds that things get dirty, and is mildly surprised to find the whole enterprise costs less than he expected.

---

## What Ned Wears

**Grey trousers** — plain, slightly stiff when new, softening over the first few weeks. The kind that every English primary school boy has worn for generations. Ned folds them each evening over the chair in his room, which Edmund notices and does not comment on.

**White polo shirt** — two of them, identical. Washed and dried on the range in winter. In the first week of term he comes home with a small ink mark on the left cuff that will not quite come out.

**Navy school sweatshirt** — with the school's name and a small logo embroidered on the chest in white. This is the uniform item that belongs to a specific place. Ned wears it with the sleeves pushed up slightly, which is how he wears most things.

**Black school shoes** — replacing his usual trainers for the school day. They are slightly too shiny for the first week and then become acceptably worn. He keeps them by the back door and changes into them before the bicycle ride, which Edmund also notes and does not comment on.

**The colourful socks** — these remain. Under the grey trousers, against the regulation black shoes, invisible to everyone. Ned knows they are there. This is the one item that belongs to no uniform, no school, no arrangement — simply to him, carried from house to house, continuous. The colourful socks stay.

---

## What the Uniform Does

Ned has worn his own clothes through all the houses — the faded hoodie, the dark jeans, the too-large waterproof jacket. His clothes belonged to no particular place.

A school uniform belongs to a place. It says: you are a pupil of this school, in this town. It is not Ned's style — it has no style — but it is a kind of belonging that his own clothes have never carried.

He does not think about this explicitly. But on the first morning, cycling down the lane toward Somerton in the navy sweatshirt with the school logo on the chest, he is aware that he looks like someone who goes to a particular school in a particular town. Someone who will be there next week and the week after. Someone whose presence in this place is not temporary.

He does not think about this for long. He concentrates on the lane, which is slightly slippery with dew, and arrives at school three minutes before the bell.

---

## Edmund's View

Edmund, watching Ned go from the kitchen window on the first morning, thinks two things.

The first is that the boy looks smaller in the uniform than in his own clothes, which he had not anticipated and which produces a feeling he does not immediately name.

The second is that he looks, without question, like someone who belongs here.

Edmund turns back to the kitchen and makes his tea and goes to the library, which is quieter than it has been since mid-July.
