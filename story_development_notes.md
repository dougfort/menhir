# Story Development Notes

## Overview

A fictional story with fantasy elements, aimed at intelligent grade school children ages eight through twelve. Tone: **cozy and mysterious**. A feeling of wonder and awe — not horror. The central presence of the standing stone should feel ancient and watchful, not threatening.

### Central Characters
- A middle-aged man (protagonist)
- A preteen boy (protagonist)
- An ancient standing stone (central presence)

---

## Location

### Region
**Somerset, England** — south of the Mendip Hills. Rolling farmland, old orchards, sunken lanes with banks so old they predate the Romans. Villages with ancient, half-dreaming names.

### The Nearby Town
**Somerton** (or a town very like it) — an ancient, quietly handsome market town. Once the county town of Somerset. Population in the range of three to six thousand.

- Large enough for a school, shops, a Saturday market, a football club with muddy pitches
- Small enough that a new face at school is noticed for about a week, then becomes normal
- The estate sits two or three miles outside of town
- Close enough to reach by bicycle on quiet lanes

This gives the boy a grounded, ordinary life — school, friends, chip shops, football — to contrast with the mystery of the estate and the stone.

---

## The Estate

- Modest by grand standards — perhaps forty to fifty acres
- Old money, slightly reduced circumstances
- Some land is managed; some is quietly going its own way
- A farmhouse that grew into something larger over several centuries, then slowly contracted again as rooms were closed off
- Stone walls, low ceilings in the older parts
- A kitchen with a flagstone floor worn smooth by generations of feet
- A walled kitchen garden — mostly weeds, but still producing apples and soft fruit in season
- An old orchard
- A meadow that has not been ploughed in living memory

---

## The Stone

A single **menhir** at the far edge of the meadow, half screened by a blackthorn hedge that has grown up around it over the centuries.

- **Height:** fifteen to eighteen feet tall
- **Material:** local limestone, gone dark with age and weather, patched with orange and grey lichen
- Leans very slightly — not enough to worry about, just enough to suggest it has opinions about vertical
- Has stood so long that the field has built up around its base
- No one knows how deep it goes

---

## Story Possibilities

- The boy might have a school friend or two who know the estate exists but have never quite visited
- Local people may have half-remembered folklore about the stone — old jokes, things grandparents said
- The contrast between the boy's ordinary town life and the ancient mystery of the estate is a key structural element
