# Story Chronology

---

## Overview

The story's main action runs from mid-July 2026 through the autumn school term. The summer period — approximately six weeks before school begins — is the first movement. The school term is the second movement, in which the conversations deepen and the adoption question moves from background to foreground.

---

## Before Ned Arrives

**Years before the story begins**
Edmund has been visiting the stone since childhood. His occult experimentation in his twenties. His return to Venn Court. Thirty years of early morning visits, careful observation, private notes never written down.

**Approximately eight years before**
Ned is born. His family circumstances become difficult very early.

**Ned, age approximately six — House One (Aunt and Uncle)**
The fish begin dying. The uncle's watch stops. A houseplant drops all its leaves. Nobody connects this to Ned. The arrangement ends.

**Ned, age approximately seven — House Two (Older Cousins)**
Electrical incidents — the television, the wifi router, the older cousin's phone losing all its photographs. The lamp in the hallway outside Ned's room. Nobody blames Ned. The arrangement ends. The older cousin notices, once, that the incidents stopped when Ned left. She does not mention this again.

**Ned, age approximately eight — House Three (Great-Aunt Marian)**
Small objects moving. A cup found on the windowsill. The photograph of the husband turning to face the wrong direction. Marian looks in Ned's notebook and finds a map of her house drawn with details Ned could not have known. She tells no one. She asks for Ned to be moved. She still feels guilty. She sends cards.

**The letter that reaches Edmund**
From a solicitor or intermediary. Formal, carefully understated. There is a boy. He is family, after a fashion. There is nowhere else. Edmund sits with it for several days. Visits the stone. Says yes.

## Movement One — The Summer

### Mid-July 2026 — Ned Arrives

**The journey**
Ned travels alone from London Paddington to Castle Cary. The Somerset Levels seen from the train window — his first impression of somewhere old. Edmund on the platform with a piece of paper that says NED.

**Chips at Castle Cary**
Edmund notices Ned's slight undernourishment and suggests the chip shop without making anything of it. Their first conversation — the Levels, *it feels older*, *no one does, entirely.*

**Arriving at Venn Court**
The lane narrowing. The house. The kitchen. Ned's room in the Georgian part of the house. The three books on the shelf — *The Little Prince*, *The Wind in the Willows*, *Treasure Island*. Ned going to the books before the window. The backpack set down on the bed.

### Days 1-7 — Settling In

The bedtime reading ritual established on the first night — Edmund noticing Ned's watchfulness, offering *The Little Prince*, the book passed back and forth, Ned falling asleep mid-passage. The ritual becomes fixed.

Ned drying up without being asked, from the second morning. Edmund noting this.

The kitchen range, the kitchen table, the flagstone floor. The house making its sounds at night. Ned's watchfulness gradually loosening — Venn Court the first place where the familiar dread does not arrive.

Edmund's early signs noted privately: the kitchen lamp flickering near Ned's usual place. His watch beginning to lose time.

### Week 2 — Into the World

**The walk to Somerton**
Edmund invites Ned on his morning walk. Mrs. Perrett in the baker's — *Kelway, is it?* — the pastry without being asked. Derek in the hardware shop. Ned seeing local children on bicycles. Edmund asking around, finding the green bicycle, the helmet from Derek's shop. The ride home — Ned circling back to stay with Edmund, unable quite to leave him behind.

**The kitchen garden**
Edmund mentions the beans look ready. Vilmorin-Andrieux produced from the library and handed over with complete seriousness. Ned takes it into the garden under his arm. The chores beginning to establish themselves — weeding, watering, harvesting. The kitchen garden gradually becoming partly Ned's territory.

**Somerton routine establishing**
The library at Cox's Yard. Miss Pardew, who recognises a serious reader. The local children — Will Haddon especially. Ned absorbing the stone's local mythology: midnight rituals, treasure, the man who talks to the dead. Will's gran who told him not to go near it.

### Day ~14 — The First Visit to the Stone

Ned has seen Edmund go to the meadow several times. One morning he asks: *Where do you go?*

The walk through the orchard. The stone appearing above the meadow grass. The grass thinning on approach. The warmth — unexpected, dry while everything else is damp. Ned noticing that nothing is wrong: the strangest place he has ever been and no familiar dread.

The compass: not pointing north. Ned's thought that this might be something he could show Edmund — filed away, not yet ready.

Edmund's monologue — the first time he has said any of it aloud. *I think some trace remains. Not the meaning. The meaning is gone.* Ned's response: *I think you're right.* The specific quality of certainty Edmund hears in it.

*I don't know yet* — and Edmund hearing the difference between that and *I don't know.*

Edmund's unpractised hand on Ned's shoulder. The walk back through the orchard.

Later: Ned drawing the stone in his notebook. Then *Venn Court* written at the top of a fresh page in careful letters.

### Days ~17-18 — The Ceremony

An ordinary day. Aunt Marian's letter at breakfast — Ned volunteering this without being asked, a small new thing.

Edmund working on his manuscript in the morning. Taking down *Ornaments in Jade* from the shelf. Carrying it to the kitchen garden where Ned is thinning carrots. *This is a Machen story about a standing stone. Short. You might find it interesting.*

Ned reading it during the afternoon. Returning it to the library that evening.

*She was afraid of it.* / *Yes.* / *I wasn't.*

*It felt old. Not frightening. Just — very old. Like it had been waiting. Patient.*

Edmund: *That's closer to what I think than anything I've read. Including things I've written myself.*

*Is there more?* / *There's rather a lot more.*

Edmund takes down Blackwood's *The Tarn of Sacrifice*. *Start at page sixty-seven. Stranger than the Machen. But more recognisable, I think.*

Dinner: slow-roasted lamb. Ned mentioning he could smell it from the garden from four o'clock. A small spontaneous observation, offered without being asked.

### Days ~21-25 — Blackwood and the Second Stone Visit

Ned reads *The Tarn of Sacrifice*. The line that stays with him: *I am glad that you have come at last. We have been expecting you.*

A second visit to the stone — possibly a third. The compass shown to Edmund without words, simply held out.

*How long has it done that?* / *Only here.* / *You've checked it elsewhere?* / *Yes.*

Edmund nods. He is thinking of his watch. The kitchen lamp. He says none of this yet.

A conversation beginning to form about what Edmund has noticed since Ned arrived. Not yet. But forming.

### Day ~28 — Edmund Asks About School

Over tea. Edmund's careful framing: *I'd like to register you at the primary school in Somerton. For September. If you're agreeable.*

Ned's question that is not quite a question: *What happens at the end of summer?*

Edmund's answer: *I am not registering you at a school in order to remove you from it in July.*

Ned's *all right* — quiet and considered. His *thank you*, later, said to his tea rather than to Edmund's face.

### Days ~30-35 — Edmund's Equanimity Opens the Door

*Doesn't it bother you? The compass.*

Edmund's response: *Strange things happen near the stone. I've observed this for thirty years. What else have you observed?*

Ned's first small disclosure: *Sometimes things move. Objects. When I'm in a particular kind of place.*

*And this place?* / A long pause. / *This place feels right.*

Edmund noting what he has observed since Ned arrived — the lamp, his watch — described as observations, not accusations. Ned: *You noticed.*

## Movement Two — The School Term

### Early September — School Begins

Ned starts Year 5 at the Somerton primary school. A new rhythm: the bicycle ride to school, the school day, coming home. Edmund's mornings alone again, as before, except not quite as before.

Ned encountering Somerton differently — as a school rather than a summer destination. The local children becoming classmates rather than summer acquaintances. A friendship beginning, perhaps, with someone specific.

The bedtime reading ritual continues. *The Little Prince* has been finished. Other books begun.

### September Onwards — Conversations Continuing

The deeper conversations arise in the new rhythm — evenings, weekends, walks. More of Ned's history emerging in small stages, the wall he has maintained for as long as he can remember coming down gradually and without drama.

Edmund's private theory solidifying. His thinking about what to do with it — practically, legally, in terms of the adoption question — moving from background toward something he will have to address.

The question of what the right book about the stone might be. Edmund beginning to think about this differently.

---

## Movement Three — The Birthday and Beyond

### Mid to Late October 2026 — Ned Turns Ten

Six or seven weeks into the school term. Ned has been at Somerton primary school long enough to have made one or two genuine connections — the first friendships with actual history.

Edmund asks, carefully, whether Ned would like anyone to come to Venn Court for his birthday. He does not assume. He does not make it a production. Ned names someone — whoever he names tells Edmund something important about who Ned is becoming in Somerton.

Edmund organises the party with characteristic seriousness and mild bafflement. Good food. A cake, baked with focused attention, which turns out surprisingly well. The house made available in a way it hasn't been before — children in the kitchen, the yard, the orchard. Edmund somewhere in the background, finding it not unpleasant.

A child from school standing in the kitchen at Venn Court, meeting Edmund, glimpsing the meadow through the window and asking what that stone is. The ordinary world and the strange world touching for the first time.

The birthday marks the end of the second movement and the beginning of the third. By this point:
- The adoption question is moving toward something formal
- Ned has disclosed more of his history to Edmund
- The stone has become shared territory
- The two worlds — the estate and Somerton — are beginning to connect

---

## Horizon — What the Story Is Moving Toward

- The adoption question moving from Edmund's private thinking to something that requires action
- Ned's history fully disclosed to Edmund — not all at once, not dramatically
- A deeper engagement with the stone — what it does differently when Ned is present
- The right book, and whose it is to write
- Ned beginning a school term knowing, for the first time, that he will finish it in the same place he started it
